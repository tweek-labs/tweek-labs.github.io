Tweek
Kompress

  • A base layer (aka compression layer or skin) from Tweek, which is a figure fitting garment worn inside the jersey. Kompress comes as a top and a lower to cover the full body of the player.
  • Kompress has locations throughout the body where a Tweek Lambda Module can be placed to begin motion tracking of a player.
  • Kompress provides thermal and moisture regulation by taking away sweat from the body during practice sessions and physical activity. Dry skin makes sure that the player doesn’t feel irritation in their skin during or after the game.
  • Kompress is made to keep muscles compressed during and after practice sessions. This compression has been proven by research to relieve muscle pain and fatigue by maintaining the heat released by the body.
  • Kompress reduces the risk of injuries during and after practice sessions because of the properties of thermal regulation and muscle compression.

Mixed Action

We can detect on the field if a player is repeating a mixed action rather than a pure side on or front on action. A mixed action can be lethal for the player’s career and we believe it is crucial to detect such harmful action early on in a player’s career.

Balance

The movement of the body and the centre of gravity for a batsman and a bowler determines the control they have over the rest of the limbs. We give insights about the balance and momentum of the player in different parts of the game.

Head Stability

During batting and bowling, the stability of the head is something extremely essential for the balance of the body and control over the delivery or the swing of the bat. We give the head movement analysis of the player in the duration of their session and give overall feedback about the player’s head movement throughout the session.

Footwork

Feet movement of a player determines the rhythm and speed of a fast bowler. For a batsman, the footwork helps them transfer their weight towards the delivery and helps them maintain balance and control over it. We give analysis of the foot movement based on the context of bowling and batting and its implication on the performance of the player.
